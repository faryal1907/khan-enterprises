# **Part A - Complete Financial Flow**

This section traces one full business cycle from the owner's initial investment through to the final state of the books. Every journal entry shown here is auto-generated by the system.

## **Stage 1 - Owner Invests**

The owner deposits Rs. 5,000,000 into the business bank account. This is the starting point of all capital.

| **Entry**        | **Debit (Dr)** | **Credit (Cr)**         |
| ---------------- | -------------- | ----------------------- |
| Owner investment | Bank 5,000,000 | Owner Capital 5,000,000 |

_Bank (asset) increases. Owner Capital (equity) increases - the business now owes this amount back to the owner._

## **Stage 2 - Purchase Order Raised**

Admin raises a PO for 1 × Honda CD70 at Rs. 180,000. No accounting entry is created - a PO is just a record of intent.

## **Stage 3 - Bikes Received (Credit Purchase)**

Honda delivers the bike. The business takes the stock but has not paid yet.

| **Entry**         | **Debit (Dr)**    | **Credit (Cr)**          |
| ----------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------ |
| Receive inventory | Inventory 180,000 | Accounts Payable 180,000 |

_Inventory (asset) increases. Accounts Payable (liability) increases - business now owes Honda Rs. 180,000._

## **Stage 4 - Partial Payment to Honda**

Admin pays Rs. 100,000 to Honda from the bank. Rs. 80,000 still outstanding.

| **Entry**        | **Debit (Dr)**           | **Credit (Cr)** |
| ---------------- | ------------------------ | --------------- |
| Supplier payment | Accounts Payable 100,000 | Bank 100,000    |

_Liability reduces by Rs. 100,000. Bank reduces by Rs. 100,000._

## **Stage 5 - Sell the Bike (Partial Customer Payment)**

CD70 sold to Ali for Rs. 200,000. Ali pays Rs. 50,000 now and Rs. 150,000 later.

| **Entry**               | **Debit (Dr)**                          | **Credit (Cr)**       |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Record sale             | Cash 50,000 Accounts Receivable 150,000 | Sales Revenue 200,000 |
| Remove inventory (COGS) | Cost of Goods Sold 180,000              | Inventory 180,000     |

_Revenue is recorded in full at the point of sale regardless of how much cash was received. The bike's cost moves from Inventory into COGS. The unpaid Rs. 150,000 sits in Accounts Receivable._

## **Stage 6 - Collect Remaining from Ali**

Ali pays the remaining Rs. 150,000.

| **Entry**       | **Debit (Dr)** | **Credit (Cr)**             |
| --------------- | -------------- | --------------------------- |
| Collect balance | Cash 150,000   | Accounts Receivable 150,000 |

_Accounts Receivable clears to zero. Cash increases._

## **Stage 7 - Pay Remaining Balance to Honda**

Admin pays the remaining Rs. 80,000 owed to Honda.

| **Entry**              | **Debit (Dr)**          | **Credit (Cr)** |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------- | --------------- |
| Final supplier payment | Accounts Payable 80,000 | Bank 80,000     |

_Accounts Payable fully cleared. Honda is owed nothing._

## **Stage 8 - Pay Salaries**

Monthly salaries of Rs. 85,000 paid from cash.

| **Entry**      | **Debit (Dr)**        | **Credit (Cr)** |
| -------------- | --------------------- | --------------- |
| Salary expense | Salary Expense 85,000 | Cash 85,000     |

## **End State - What the Books Show**

After all eight stages, every account settles as follows.

| **Account**         | **Category** | **Calculation**                                            | **Balance**   |
| ------------------- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
| Cash                | Asset        | 50,000 (sale) + 150,000 (collection) − 85,000 (salary)     | Rs. 115,000   |
| Bank                | Asset        | 5,000,000 (investment) − 100,000 − 80,000 (Honda payments) | Rs. 4,820,000 |
| Inventory           | Asset        | Received 180,000 → moved to COGS on sale                   | Rs. 0         |
| Accounts Receivable | Asset        | 150,000 created → 150,000 collected                        | Rs. 0         |
| Accounts Payable    | Liability    | 180,000 created → 180,000 paid in full                     | Rs. 0         |
| Owner Capital       | Equity       | Owner's initial investment                                 | Rs. 5,000,000 |
| Sales Revenue       | Revenue      | One bike sold at 200,000                                   | Rs. 200,000   |
| Cost of Goods Sold  | Expense      | Purchase cost of sold bike                                 | Rs. 180,000   |
| Salary Expense      | Expense      | July salaries                                              | Rs. 85,000    |

|              |                                        |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| Gross Profit | Rs. 200,000 − Rs. 180,000 = Rs. 20,000 |
| Net Profit   | Rs. 20,000 − Rs. 85,000 = − Rs. 65,000 |

_The business is cash-rich (Rs. 4.9M in bank and cash) but shows a net loss this period because the salary expense outweighed the margin on a single bike sale. This is expected at low sales volumes - the books are correct._

# **Part B - Admin Module UX Plan**

_Admin-only. The module behaves like a specialized ERP spreadsheet - every table is editable, filterable, sortable, and exportable. Journal entries are auto-generated on every financial event and are always visible for review. The admin has full manual override on any record._

| **Core Behaviour** | **Detail**                                                                  |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Inline editing     | Click any editable cell to modify directly - no separate form pages         |
| Auto-accounting    | PO receipts, payments, sales, and collections all auto-post journal entries |
| Manual override    | Admin can add, edit, or void any record at any time                         |
| Linked records     | Every row links to its source document for full drill-down                  |
| Bulk actions       | Select multiple rows to post, void, export, or reassign in one action       |
| Audit trail        | Every change logs the user, timestamp, and previous value                   |

## **Module sections**

| **Section**       | **Purpose**                                                             |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dashboard         | Live financial snapshot - all figures drill into their source table     |
| Purchase Orders   | Raise and manage POs for bikes and parts; track supplier payment status |
| Payables          | All money owed - suppliers, expenses, salaries, loans                   |
| Sales Invoices    | Customer invoices - record sales and collect payments                   |
| Receivables       | All outstanding customer balances and collections                       |
| Journal Entries   | Full auto and manual ledger - source of truth for all reports           |
| Chart of Accounts | Master account list referenced by journal entries                       |
| Bank & Cash       | CRUD accounts (bank, cash, e-wallets); transfers and reconciliation     |
| Reports           | P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, Trial Balance, Aging, Statements         |

## **Admin controls**

- New manual entry - add rows directly; account field autocompletes from Chart of Accounts

- Filter - by date range, account, source (auto / manual), status
- Source link - click any Source cell to open the originating PO, invoice, or payment

## **CRUD controls**

- Add account - new row inline; set Name, Type (Bank / Cash / E-wallet), Account No. / ID, and Opening Balance
- Edit - update name, account number, or type at any time
- Deactivate - hides from payment method pickers across the module; balance preserved; can be reactivated

## **Per-account actions**

- Transactions - full transaction history for that account with running balance
- Transfer - move funds between any two accounts; From / To / Amount / Date / Reference; auto-journal posted
- Reconcile - match system transactions against bank or e-wallet statement line by line; flag unmatched entries
- Manual entry - record bank charges, interest, e-wallet fees, or any account-initiated item
